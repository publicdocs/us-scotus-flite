    HAMILTON V. ALABAMA [376 U.S. 650][/us/courts/scotus/usReporter/376/650]

    NO. 793.  DECIDED MARCH 30, 1964.  - CERTIORARI GRANTED AND JUDGMENT REVERSED.  - REPORTED BELOW:  275 ALA. 574, 156 SO. 2D 926.

    HAMILTON V. ALABAMA.

    ON PETITION FOR WRIT OF CERTIORARI TO THE SUPREME COURT OF ALABAMA.

    PER CURIAM.

    THE PETITION FOR WRIT OF CERTIORARI IS GRANTED.  THE JUDGMENT IS REVERSED.  JOHNSON V. VIRGINIA, [373 U.S. 61][/us/courts/scotus/usReporter/373/61].

    MR. JUSTICE BLACK CONCURS IN REVERSAL OF THE JUDGMENT OF CONTEMPT FOR REASONS DISCUSSED IN IN RE MURCHISON, [349 U.S. 133][/us/courts/scotus/usReporter/349/133], IN RE OLIVER, [333 U.S. 257][/us/courts/scotus/usReporter/333/257], AND THOMPSON V. CITY OF LOUISVILLE, [362 U.S. 199][/us/courts/scotus/usReporter/362/199].  CF. OFFUTT V. UNITED STATES, [348 U.S. 11][/us/courts/scotus/usReporter/348/11].

    MR. JUSTICE CLARK, MR. JUSTICE HARLAN AND MR. JUSTICE WHITE ARE OF THE OPINION THAT CERTIORARI SHOULD BE DENIED.
