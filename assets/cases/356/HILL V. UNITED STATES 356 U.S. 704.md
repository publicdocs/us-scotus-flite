    NO. 287, MISC.  DECIDED JUNE 2, 1958 - REPORTED BELOW:  SEE 101 U.S. APP. D.C. 313, 248 F.2D 635.

    CERTIORARI AND LEAVE TO PROCEED IN FORMA PAUPERIS GRANTED; JUDGMENT VACATED AND CASE REMANDED FOR CONSIDERATION IN LIGHT OF CASES CITED.

    HILL V. UNITED STATES.

    ON PETITION FOR WRIT OF CERTIORARI TO THE UNITED STATES COURT OF APPEALS FOR THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA CIRCUIT.

    PER CURIAM.

    THE MOTION FOR LEAVE TO PROCEED IN FORMA PAUPERIS AND THE PETITION FOR WRIT OF CERTIORARI ARE GRANTED.  THE JUDGMENT OF THE UNITED STATES COURT OF APPEALS FOR THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA CIRCUIT IS VACATED AND THE CASE IS REMANDED TO THAT COURT FOR CONSIDERATION IN LIGHT OF ELLIS V. UNITED STATES, [356 U.S. 674][/us/courts/scotus/usReporter/356/674], DECIDED MAY 26, 1958, AND ALSO IN LIGHT OF FARLEY V. UNITED STATES, [354 U.S. 521][/us/courts/scotus/usReporter/354/521]; AND JOHNSON V. UNITED STATES, [352 U.S. 565][/us/courts/scotus/usReporter/352/565].
