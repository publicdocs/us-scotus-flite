    NO. 83, MISC.  DECIDED OCTOBER 14, 1963.  - CERTIORARI GRANTED; JUDGMENT VACATED; AND CASE REMANDED.

    TABB V. CALIFORNIA.

    ON PETITION FOR WRIT OF CERTIORARI TO THE SUPREME COURT OF CALIFORNIA.

    PER CURIAM.

    THE MOTION FOR LEAVE TO PROCEED IN FORMA PAUPERIS AND THE PETITION FOR A WRIT OF CERTIORARI ARE GRANTED.  THE JUDGMENT IS VACATED AND THE CASE IS REMANDED TO THE SUPREME COURT OF CALIFORNIA FOR FURTHER CONSIDERATION IN LIGHT OF DOUGLAS V. CALIFORNIA, [372 U.S. 353][/us/courts/scotus/usReporter/372/353].

    MR. JUSTICE HARLAN, FOR THE REASONS STATED IN DAEGELE V. KANSAS, ANTE, P. 1, WOULD HAVE WITHHELD DISPOSITION OF THIS PETITION FOR CERTIORARI UNTIL THE DISPOSITION, AFTER ARGUMENT, OF THAT CASE.
