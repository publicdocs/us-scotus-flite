    CARLOS V. NEW YORK, [396 U.S. 119][/us/courts/scotus/usReporter/396/119]

    CARLOS V. NEW YORK ON PETITION FOR WRIT OF CERTIORARI TO THE COURT OF APPEALS OF NEW YORK NO. 524.  DECIDED DECEMBER 8, 1969

    CERTIORARI GRANTED; 24 N.Y.2D 865, 248 N.E.2D 924, REVERSED.

    PER CURIAM.

    THE PETITION FOR A WRIT OF CERTIORARI IS GRANTED AND THE JUDGMENT IS REVERSED, REDRUP V. NEW YORK, [386 U.S. 767][/us/courts/scotus/usReporter/386/767].

    THE CHIEF JUSTICE AND MR. JUSTICE HARLAN ARE OF THE OPINION THAT CERTIORARI SHOULD BE DENIED.  HOWEVER, THE CASE HAVING BEEN TAKEN FOR REVIEW, THEY WOULD AFFIRM THE JUDGMENT OF THE STATE COURT UPON THE PREMISES STATED IN MR. JUSTICE HARLAN'S SEPARATE OPINION IN ROTH V. UNITED STATES, [354 U.S. 476][/us/courts/scotus/usReporter/354/476], 496 (1957), AND IN HIS DISSENTING OPINION IN MEMOIRS V. MASSACHUSETTS, [383 U.S. 413][/us/courts/scotus/usReporter/383/413], 455 (1966).

    HERALD PRICE FAHRINGER AND EUGENE GRESSMAN FOR PETITIONER.
